## Context Questions

**1. Organization**
- What company or industry is this? (e.g., financial services, telco, utilities, retail, healthcare, government)
- Roughly how large is the organization — staff headcount and customer base?
- Is this a single business unit or an enterprise-wide program?

**2. What's already known or suspected**
- Do you have a hypothesis about *why* resolution time is 15 days? Is it a process bottleneck, a system constraint, an escalation problem, or something else?
- What's driving the complaints — do you know the top complaint categories? Or is that part of what needs to be understood?
- Have there been previous attempts to fix this? If so, what happened?

**3. Materials you can share**
Any of the following would significantly improve the quality of the blocker scan. Paste them directly into the chat or upload the documents:

| Material | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Customer complaint data or categories | Tells me where resolution is actually breaking down |
| Interview or survey notes from staff or customers | Surface the real blockers, not the official version |
| Process documentation or flowcharts | Identifies where the 15-day journey actually lives |
| Org chart or description of team structure | Reveals ownership gaps and handoff problems |
| NPS, CSAT, or VOC data | Shows where experience failure is concentrated |
| Any prior diagnostic, audit, or review outputs | Avoids re-covering ground already documented |
| Outputs from `/biz-context` or `/journey-map` if you've run those | Feed directly into this scan |

**4. Scope**
- Which customer segment or journey type are we scanning for? (All complaints? A specific product line? A specific channel?)
- Are there complaints or resolution types that are explicitly out of scope — e.g., regulatory escalations, fraud cases?

Once I have this, I'll work systematically through a structured taxonomy of 8 blocker categories — covering culture and leadership, org structure, process and operations, technology and data, people and capability, regulatory and compliance, and market and economic factors.

The output will be:
- A full **blocker map** with severity and addressability ratings for each blocker found
- A **"Where to Start"** recommendation identifying the 3 highest-leverage blockers to address first
- A **Landmines** section covering blockers that look manageable but could derail the program
- A **Data Gaps** section flagging areas I couldn't assess with available information

The more specific the inputs, the more specific and actionable the blocker map will be. If you have nothing written down yet, that's fine — we can work from a verbal description of the situation and I'll ask structured follow-up questions as we go.
