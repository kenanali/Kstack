## Four questions I need you to answer

Before I begin clustering, I need to understand the context:

**1. What are you synthesising this for?**
The same research produces different synthesis outputs depending on purpose. Examples:
- "Run an ideation workshop with our design team"
- "Write a board paper justifying CX investment"
- "Brief a design sprint team"
- "Prepare for a leadership alignment session"
- "Build a business case"

**2. What is the specific CX challenge or strategic question?**
What problem are you actually trying to answer? The more specific the better. A vague question produces a vague synthesis. Examples:
- "Why are customers leaving after their first year despite high acquisition rates?"
- "Where should we invest design effort to reduce cost-to-serve without degrading experience?"
- "What's driving the gap between our digital satisfaction scores and our overall NPS?"

**3. Which research outputs are you bringing in?**
List what you have, even if it's informal. Tell me the source and roughly what it covers.

**4. Is there anything you already know that you want the synthesis to either confirm or challenge?**
If your team already has a hypothesis about what's causing the problem, tell me. I'll either find evidence that supports it, evidence that contradicts it, or both. I won't just validate what you already believe.
