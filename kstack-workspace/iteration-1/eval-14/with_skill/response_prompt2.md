# Workshop Design: Intake Response
## EVAL-15 | workshop-prep | Prompt 2

---

To design a workshop that actually works, I need six things. Without them, I'm designing a generic agenda rather than a session for specific humans with a specific problem to solve.

---

**1. Objective — what specifically must be true at the end?**

Not "shared understanding" or "alignment." A real output:
- "A prioritized shortlist of 3–5 ideas to take forward"
- "A decision on which of these two options to pursue"
- "A journey map that the whole team accepts as the current-state baseline"

If you can't define what "done" looks like, the workshop will feel successful and produce nothing.

**2. Audience — who's in the room?**

- How many people?
- What are their roles and seniority levels?
- How familiar are they with CX methods — have they done journey mapping, HMW exercises, ideation sprints before?
- Are there any dynamics I should know about — a person who tends to dominate, a team that's been in conflict, anyone who's skeptical of the whole exercise?

Workshop design is fundamentally about calibrating to the actual humans in the room. A session for 4 senior executives runs completely differently from a session for 12 service designers.

**3. Time available**

2 hours, half-day, full day? And is that time fixed, or is there flex?

**4. Research inputs — what do participants need to respond to?**

- What insights, findings, tensions, or customer evidence should the workshop be built around?
- Do participants already know this material, or does the session need to create shared context first?
- If you have outputs from `/synthesize`, `/journey-map`, or `/biz-context`, paste or upload them and I'll use them directly.
- If you don't have documents, describe the key problem in a few sentences and I'll ask sharper follow-up questions.

**5. Format — in-person, hybrid, or fully remote?**

This changes everything about activity design. Hybrid is the hardest to run well and requires specific choices.

**6. Constraints — anything I should know?**

- A decision that's already made (so we don't accidentally re-litigate it)
- A political dynamic I need to design around
- Limited materials or technology
- A hard end time that people will leave for regardless of where we are in the agenda

---

Once I have these six inputs, I'll confirm the objective framing with you before designing — to make sure we're building toward the right output, not just a well-run session.
