# Business Case: Onboarding Experience Improvement
## EVAL-17 | biz-case | Prompt 2

---

Yes — and to build one that will actually hold up with a CFO or Investment Committee, I need six inputs. Here's what I'm looking for and why each matters:

---

**1. Initiative name and description — what specifically are you proposing to change?**

"Improving our onboarding experience" is a category, not an initiative. I need to understand the scope:
- Is this a digital onboarding flow (online sign-up, ID verification, account activation)?
- Is this a human-assisted onboarding process (a consultant, branch, or call centre guiding customers through setup)?
- Is it both?
- What's the current state you're starting from, and what does "improved" mean — faster, less friction, fewer drop-offs, better-informed customers?

The more specific the scope, the more credible the business case.

**2. Audience for the business case — who are you presenting to?**

A CFO wants ROI logic, payback period, and cost certainty.
An Investment Committee wants strategic alignment, risk register, and implementation confidence.
A Board wants one page with a clear recommendation at the top.

Each requires a different emphasis. Tell me who's reading this and what they'll push back on.

**3. Investment ask — what are you expecting this to cost?**

Even a rough number helps. Is this a $200K project, a $2M program, or a $10M transformation? The investment scale determines how much rigour the value case needs and what level of ROI is credible.

**4. Existing numbers — what data do you have?**

The more data you can share, the less I have to estimate. Useful inputs include:
- Current onboarding completion rate (what % of customers who start, finish?)
- Drop-off rate and where it happens in the journey
- Time to complete onboarding (current state)
- Customer satisfaction scores for the onboarding experience (CSAT, NPS at onboarding, complaints)
- 90-day retention rate for newly onboarded customers vs. benchmark
- Current cost to onboard one customer (if known)
- Volume: how many customers go through onboarding per year?

If you don't have all of these, share what you have. I'll flag what I'm estimating vs. what's known.

**5. Available inputs — do you have any existing research or analysis?**

If you have outputs from `/synthesize`, `/journey-map`, `/biz-context`, or `/scan-blockers` for the onboarding journey, paste or upload them. I'll use them directly. If you have NPS data, verbatim complaints, or a journey map, share those too.

If you don't have documents — describe the key problems you know exist in the onboarding experience and I'll work with that.

**6. Timeline — when do you need this delivered, and what's the program timeline?**

This matters for the payback calculation and the implementation roadmap.

---

Once I have these inputs, I'll flag any gaps before building the full case — so we agree on what's data, what's estimated, and what's assumption. The CFO will ask about this, so we might as well get it right before the room.
